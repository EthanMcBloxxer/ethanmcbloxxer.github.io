We use Amplitude Analytics.
It collects your country (such as The US), language (such as English), and OS platform/carrier (such as Windows or iPhone/Verizon) anonymously.
This data is only kept on Amplitude's servers for 90 days, then removed.

It does also create a cookie with your device ID and another encrypted string (and possibly other cookies).
You can use Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control to opt-out, as we only load the analytics with both of these disabled. You can also use the "do not track" button in the footer on certain pages, or do amplitude.getInstance().setOptOut(true); in the console.

You can enable Do Not Track with these methods:

Chrome and Chromium-based browsers, like Opera and Brave
  1. Open your settings page
  2. Turn on Send a "Do not track" request with your browsing traffic
Chromium Package
  1. Open your settings page
  2. Click More
  3. Turn on Send a "Do Not Track" request with your browsing traffic
Firefox and Firefox Profiles, like Tor Browser
  1. Open your settings page
  2. Scroll down to Send websites a "Do Not Track" signal that you don't want to be tracked
  3. Toggle Always
Microsoft Edge
  1. Open your settings page
  2. Scroll down to Privacy
  3. Turn on Send "Do Not Track" requests
Internet Explorer
  1. Click on the gear near the top right
  2. Hover over Safety
  3. Click Turn on Do Not Track requests
Other Browsers We don't have every browser here, so please look up how to turn on Do Not Track requests for your browser, or look in its settings. If you can't find anything about it, try setting navigator.doNotTrack to "1" in its source code or flag settings.

And to turn on Global Privacy Control, go to the official website and download one of the supporting extensions or browsers.
Like Do Not Track, you can also set navigator.globalPrivacyControl to "1" if none of these extensions or browsers appeal to you.

As of right now, deleting your data prior to the 90-day expiration is only possible with your Amplitude ID or Amplitude Device ID.
